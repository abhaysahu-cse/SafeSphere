1. What is a Heatwave?

A heatwave is a period of unusually high temperatures, often with high humidity, lasting several days. Heatwaves raise the risk of heat exhaustion, heatstroke and dehydration — especially for children and older adults.

2. Causes & Risk Factors

  • Prolonged high temperatures and heat trapped by urban areas
  • High humidity which reduces cooling by sweat
  • Outdoor activities during peak heat hours (10 AM–4 PM)

3. Before & During a Heatwave — Preparation

Home & School Preparation

  • Plan outdoor activities in the morning or late afternoon
  • Set shaded rest areas and provide extra water stations
  • Encourage loose, light-colored clothing and hats
  • Know signs of heat illness and first-aid steps

Cooling Kit

Water bottles
Electrolyte drinks (ORS)
Wet towels / spray bottle
Hat / umbrella
Sunscreen
Schools: consider a "heat action day" plan to reschedule sports and protect vulnerable students.

4. During Heat Exposure — Stay Safe

Mild heat stress

  • Stop activity, move to shade/cool area
  • Give cool water slowly, loosen tight clothing
  • Cool the body with wet towels or misting

Signs of heatstroke (SEVERE)

  • High body temperature (above 40°C), confusion, fainting
  • Hot, dry skin or very heavy sweating
  • If suspected: call emergency services immediately and cool the person while waiting.

Special guidance

  • Keep infants and elderly in cool places
  • Reschedule outdoor drills during heatwaves
