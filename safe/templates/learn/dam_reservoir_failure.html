1. What is Dam / Reservoir Failure?

Dam failure occurs when a dam collapses or releases water uncontrollably due to structural damage, overtopping, earthquakes, or sabotage. The resulting flood wave moves extremely fast downstream.

Key Fact: Flood waves from dam failure can travel faster than vehicles within the first 10–30 km.

2. Early Warning Signs

  • Sudden increase in water discharge
  • Sirens or SMS alerts from authorities
  • Cracks, seepage, or vibrations near dams
  • Unusual water color or debris

3. Rapid Downstream Evacuation

Immediate Action: Move perpendicular to the river flow toward high ground. Do NOT attempt to cross bridges.

Evacuate on foot if traffic congestion occurs. Follow pre-marked evacuation routes.

4. Mapping Safe High Ground

Communities must identify elevated zones above predicted flood levels.

  • Schools on hills
  • Concrete multi-storey buildings
  • Natural ridges and embankments

5. Community Early-Warning Roles

  • Spotters: Monitor dam status
  • Messengers: Spread alerts door-to-door
  • Guides: Lead evacuation routes
  • Care Teams: Help elderly & disabled
