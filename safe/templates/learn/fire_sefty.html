🔥 Fire Safety — Prevention, Response & Recovery

Objective: Learn how fires start, how to prevent them, how to evacuate safely, and how to use an extinguisher correctly.

Module • Fire Est. time: 10–18 min Target: Students age 10+

Hook — imagine a kitchen fire

You're cooking and a pan catches fire. Smoke builds up fast. What would you do in the first 30 seconds to protect yourself and others?

1. What is Fire?

Fire is combustion — a chemical reaction that needs fuel, heat, and oxygen. Removing one element of the triangle stops the fire.

  • Fuel: wood, paper, cloth, petrol
  • Heat: spark, friction, matchstick
  • Oxygen: air

2. Common Causes of Fire

  • Electrical faults and short circuits
  • Gas leaks and unattended cooking
  • Candles, incense sticks and smoking indoors
  • Flammable liquids stored improperly
  • Overheating devices

3. Classes of Fire

Know the class so you can choose the right extinguisher:

  • Class A: Wood, paper, cloth
  • Class B: Petrol, oil, paint
  • Class C: LPG, CNG, gas
  • Class D: Metals (magnesium, etc.)
  • Class E: Electrical fires

4. How to Use a Fire Extinguisher (PASS)

Use PASS — Pull pin, Aim low at base, Squeeze handle, Sweep side to side.

Only attempt to use an extinguisher if the fire is small and you're not in danger. If in doubt, evacuate and call emergency services.

5. Fire Prevention Tips

  • Check wiring and avoid overloading sockets
  • Keep flammable items away from heat sources
  • Install smoke detectors and test monthly
  • Keep fire extinguishers accessible and know how to use them
  • Never leave cooking unattended

6. Evacuation Steps During Fire

  • Stay calm and shout to alert others
  • Use stairs — never elevators
  • Crawl low under smoke and cover your mouth
  • Close doors behind you to slow fire spread
  • Call emergency number (e.g., 101) once safe

Activities & Drills

  3. Family plan: everyone practices escape routes at home and a meeting point.
