🔥 Fire Safety Module

Fire safety is one of the most important life-skills for students. Understanding how fires start, how they spread, and how to respond during an emergency can save your life and the lives of others. This module explains fire types, fire prevention, safe evacuation, and the correct usage of fire extinguishers.

1. What is Fire?

Fire is a chemical reaction known as combustion. For a fire to start, it needs:

  • 🔥 Fuel (wood, paper, petrol, cloth, etc.)
  • 🔥 Heat (matchstick, spark, friction)
  • 🔥 Oxygen (air)
{% comment %} {% endcomment %}

These three elements form the **Fire Triangle**. Removing even one of them will extinguish the fire. This concept is important in fire-fighting.

2. Common Causes of Fire

{% comment %} {% endcomment %}
  • ⚡ Electrical faults, short circuits
  • 🔥 Gas leaks in kitchens
  • 🕯️ Unattended candles or incense sticks
  • 🚬 Smoking indoors
  • 🛢️ Flammable liquids stored improperly
  • ⚠️ Overheated devices

3. Classes of Fire

{% comment %} {% endcomment %}

Fires are divided into categories depending on the fuel:

  • Class A: Wood, paper, cloth
  • Class B: Petrol, oil, diesel, paint
  • Class C: LPG, CNG, natural gas
  • Class D: Metals like magnesium
  • Class E: Electrical fires

Using the wrong extinguisher can cause explosions or spread the fire, so this classification is very important.

4. How to Use a Fire Extinguisher (PASS Method)

Remember the **PASS** method:

  • P – Pull the pin
  • A – Aim at the base of the fire
  • S – Squeeze the handle
  • S – Sweep from side to side

5. Fire Prevention Tips

{% comment %} {% endcomment %}
  • Check electrical wiring regularly
  • Do not overload plugs
  • Keep flammable items away from heat
  • Install smoke detectors
  • Keep fire extinguishers accessible
  • Never leave cooking unattended

6. Evacuation Steps During Fire

{% comment %} {% endcomment %}
  • Stay calm and alert others
  • Use stairs—never elevators
  • Crawl low under smoke
  • Cover mouth with a cloth
  • Follow EXIT signs
  • Call emergency number: 101
