🌪️ Advanced Tornado Safety & survival

1. Understanding Tornado Mechanics

Tornadoes are violently rotating columns of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground. They can have wind speeds over 200 mph.

The EF Scale: Tornadoes are measured by the Enhanced Fujita Scale, ranging from EF0 (minor damage) to EF5 (incredible damage).

2. Advanced Survival Tactics

  • Head Protection: Most tornado fatalities are caused by head trauma from flying debris. If you have time, put on a bicycle or sports helmet.
  • The "Triangle of Life" Myth: Experts recommend the "Duck and Cover" method under a sturdy table. Do not stand next to a void; stay under a protective structure.
  • Post-Storm Communication: Keep phone lines clear for emergency crews. Text, don't talk. Text messages often go through when voice calls fail due to network congestion.

3. Hazard Recognition

Sometimes tornadoes are "rain-wrapped" and invisible. Watch for a rotating cloud base or a "debris ball" on radar. If the sky turns a bruise-like green, a severe supercell is likely overhead.

🚨 Immediate Signs

If you hear a continuous roar like a freight train, take cover immediately. Don't wait for a siren.
