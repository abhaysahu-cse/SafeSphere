🌊 Flood Safety — Prepare, Respond & Recover

Objective: Understand flood risk, prepare an emergency plan, and act safely during flooding and flash floods.

Module • Flood Est. time: 12–20 min Target: Students age 10+

Hook — what if the water rises?

Imagine heavy rain for several hours; water starts entering ground-floor rooms and roads become impassable. Quick decisions about where to go and what to save decide safety.

What & Why — Floods in plain language

  • Floods happen when water overflows rivers, drains or coastal surge after heavy rain or storms.
  • Flash floods are sudden and fast — they can sweep away people and cars within minutes.
  • Most harm comes from being caught in moving water, contaminated water, and damaged infrastructure.

Core Safety Rule — Move to high ground & avoid moving water

  1. Move quickly to higher ground or upper floors if water encroaches.
  2. Avoid walking/driving through moving water — it may be deeper and faster than it looks.
  3. Follow local warnings and evacuation instructions from authorities.
Quick tip: 30 cm (1 foot) of moving water can knock over an adult; 60 cm can carry away most cars.

Do / Don’t

✅ DO

  • Move people to higher floors or safe points immediately.
  • Turn off electricity at main if safe and dry it off later.
  • Take essential kit (water, phone, meds) if you must move.

❌ DON’T

  • Do not walk or drive through floodwater.
  • Don’t enter flooded buildings with structural damage.
  • Never use candles in waterlogged spaces — risk of short-circuits.

Situational Tips (Home, School, Outside)

  • Home: Raise appliances, move valuables to upper rooms, switch off electricity if necessary from a dry location.
  • School: Follow evacuation route to assembly point; teachers should carry class list and first-aid kit.
  • Outside/Travel: Avoid low-lying roads; do not attempt to cross bridges or flooded fords.

After the Flood — Recovery Steps

  • Only return when authorities say it is safe.
  • Avoid floodwater — it may be contaminated and contain debris.
  • Report gas leaks, electrical faults or structural damage to officials.
  • Use boiled or bottled water until supply is declared safe.

Emergency Kit for Floods

Bottled water (3 days)
First-aid kit
Portable radio / phone power bank
Flashlight & spare batteries
Important documents in waterproof bag
Emergency blankets

Myths vs Facts

MythFact
Flood water is just dirty rainFlood water often contains sewage, chemicals, and hidden hazards.
It's OK to drive slowly through shallow waterEven shallow moving water can stall or sweep vehicles away.
Buildings float — stay upstairsIf the structure is badly damaged, upper floors may collapse; follow official advice.

Activities & Drills (Classroom / Home)

  1. Family plan: everyone practices the route to higher ground and a meeting point.
  2. Classroom drill: teachers run a timed "move to assembly" drill with roll-call.
  3. Check kit: students bring one item on the kit list and explain why it helps.
