🔥 FIRE SAFETY — Prevention & Response

Complete teaching plan with causes, prevention, response, drills, videos, posters and quizzes — ready for classroom use.

1. What Students Must Learn

A. What causes fires

Simple explanations for kids:

  • Electric short circuits — faulty wiring or overloaded sockets.
  • Gas leaks — leaking LPG/CNG combined with a spark can ignite.
  • Stove / candle accidents — unattended flames.
  • Overheating wires — old wires or overloaded plugs.
  • Careless burning — matchsticks, crackers, lit papers.

B. Fire Triangle (Very important)

Heat + Fuel + Oxygen = Fire. Remove any one and the fire dies.

C. Prevention — Everyday Safety Habits

  • Do not play with matches, lighters, or switches.
  • Switch off appliances when not in use; unplug chargers.
  • Avoid overloading plugs and extension cords.
  • Keep flammable items away from stoves and heaters.
  • Keep corridors and exits clear of clutter.
  • Teachers/maintenance should check wiring regularly.

Kitchen Safety (for older students)

  • Never leave the stove unattended.
  • Check LPG connections and use regulators properly.
  • Do NOT pour water on oil fires — use a lid or suitable extinguisher.

D. Response — What to do if fire starts

Short checklist teachers must teach and rehearse:

  • Stay calm. Alert teacher/adult and raise alarm.
  • Evacuate immediately — follow evacuation route.
  • If clothes catch fire → Stop, Drop & Roll: Stop (don’t run), Drop to ground, Roll to smother flames.
  • Stay low (smoke rises), cover nose & mouth with cloth, avoid lifts, touch doors before opening with back of hand.

E. Using Fire Extinguishers (Teens)

PASS method — Pull pin, Aim at base, Squeeze handle, Sweep side-to-side.

F. What NOT to do

  • Do NOT hide under beds.
  • Do NOT run toward smoke or flames.
  • Do NOT return inside to fetch belongings.
  • Do NOT pour water on electrical or oil fires.

4. Activities & Drills (Practical)

A. Mock Fire Drill

  1. Sound the alarm; students perform Stop, Drop & Roll if needed.
  2. Evacuate to assembly point quietly and line up.
  3. Teacher performs attendance check and reports missing students.
  4. Discuss mistakes and improvements for next drill.

Do this every 2–3 months.

B. Demonstration of Fire Extinguisher (Grades 6+)

  • Teacher demonstrates PASS with an expired/empty extinguisher or training unit.
  • Explain difference: CO₂ vs Foam vs Dry Powder.
  • Students practice pulling pin and aiming at base (simulation).

C. Role Play

Group activities where students act out scenarios: LPG leak, kitchen fire, clothes on fire, corridor smoke.

D. Drawing Activity (Younger Kids)

Topic: "How I will be safe in a fire". Display best drawings on the notice board.

5. Emergency Kit & Checklists

Basic items every classroom and home should have:

Whistle
Water bottle
First-aid
Torch
Emergency contact card
Power bank
Small snacks
Teacher checklist: Mark fire extinguisher inspection date, check alarm system, remove loose wiring and ensure exits are clear.

6. Myths vs Facts

MythFact
Stand in doorway is safestModern doorways are not engineered as shelter; find sturdy cover.
Running outside is saferMost injuries occur while running; evacuate in order.
You can predict firesPrevention is best; prediction is not possible.
Always use water on any fireWater on oil/electrical fires is dangerous; use correct extinguisher.
