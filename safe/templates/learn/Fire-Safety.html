Wildfire Safety — Prevention, Evacuation & Shelter

Objective: Understand how wildfires start, how to reduce risk, when to evacuate, and how to shelter safely.

Module • Wildfire Est. time: 12–20 min Target: Students age 12+

Hook — imagine dry hills and strong winds

You're at home near a forested area and see smoke on a windy afternoon. Within minutes the sky darkens from ash. What should you and your family do first?

1. What is a Wildfire?

Wildfire is an uncontrolled fire spreading across vegetation (grass, brush, trees). It moves quickly when conditions are dry, hot, and windy.

  • Fuels: dry grass, leaf litter, shrubs, fallen branches
  • Weather: high heat, low humidity, strong winds accelerate spread
  • Terrain: fires spread uphill faster; can jump across valleys

2. Common Causes of Wildfire

  • Human activity: unattended campfires, discarded cigarettes, sparks from machinery
  • Powerline faults and electrical equipment
  • Burning debris on windy days
  • Deliberate arson
  • Lightning strikes in dry seasons

3. How to Reduce Risk (At Home & School)

  • Create a 10–30 metre defensible space by removing dry vegetation near buildings.
  • Store firewood and flammable materials >10 m from structures.
  • Clear gutters, roofs, and verandas of leaves and debris.
  • Use spark arrestors on chimneys and exhausts; maintain machinery to prevent sparks.
  • Follow local burn bans; do not burn waste during high-risk days.
  • Install smoke alarms and keep emergency kit ready (see checklist).
Tip: Know your local wildfire alert levels and sign up for official SMS/phone alerts.

4. Evacuate or Shelter-in-Place — Decision Guide

Official instructions come from local authorities. If told to evacuate, leave immediately. If told to shelter, follow these steps.

If you are ordered to evacuate

  1. Take your emergency kit, important documents, water, and masks (N95 if available).
  2. Wear long sleeves, long pants, sturdy shoes; cover face with a damp cloth or mask.
  3. Close all windows and doors, but do not lock them; turn off gas and electricity if time permits.
  4. Follow designated evacuation routes — avoid driving through heavy smoke.
  5. Keep radio/phone on and check-in at the assembly point.

If you are instructed to shelter (stay inside)

  1. Close windows, vents, and doors; seal gaps with wet towels if smoke is heavy.
  2. Move to an interior room without windows or to a room with the fewest openings.
  3. Turn off air conditioning or forced ventilation if it draws smoke.
  4. Keep pets inside and have carriers ready.
  5. Monitor official channels for further instructions.
If you feel your home is in immediate danger — do not wait: evacuate now. Fire behaviour can change rapidly.

5. Activities & Drills

  1. Evacuation drill: timed route to a safe assembly area on low wind days; record and improve timings.
  2. Defensible space workshop: students map nearby vegetation and propose a clearing plan.
  3. Role-play: receive an evacuation order and practice packing and leaving calmly.
  4. Communication drill: practice contacting guardians and reporting attendance after evacuation.

6. Emergency Kit (Wildfire)

Keep these items in an easy-to-grab bag for every household and classroom:

N95 mask / cloth
Water (1–2 litres per person)
First-aid
Flashlight & batteries
Emergency contact list
Important documents (copies)
Basic tools / whistle
