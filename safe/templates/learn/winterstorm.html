Winter Storm Safety — Blizzard & Cold Weather Preparedness

Hook — the storm comes fast

Snow builds up, roads close and temperatures drop quickly. How will your class stay warm and safe until help arrives?

1. What is a Winter Storm / Blizzard?

Winter storms bring heavy snow, strong winds, and very cold temperatures. Blizzards reduce visibility and can cause power outages, icy roads and dangerous wind chill.

2. Causes & Risk Factors

  • Cold air masses meeting moist air
  • Rapid temperature drops increase risk of hypothermia
  • Road closures and loss of heating/power

3. Before the Storm — Preparation

Home & School Preparation

  • Keep extra blankets, warm clothing and warm drinks ready
  • Know how to safely use heaters and check CO detectors
  • Prepare an indoor shelter area and keep emergency food
  • Have a phone power bank and a hand-crank radio

Winter Kit

Blankets / sleeping bags
Warm clothing & hats
Thermos with warm drinks
Flashlight & batteries
First aid
Important: use heaters with ventilation and working carbon-monoxide detectors. Never use ovens or grills indoors for heating.

4. During the Storm — Stay Safe

Staying warm

  • Stay indoors and limit travel
  • Layer clothing and keep dry — wet clothes lose heat
  • Use shared body heat in cold emergencies (huddle)

Signs of hypothermia

  • Shivering, slow speech, clumsy movements
  • Confusion or drowsiness — seek medical help

Power outage tips

  • Keep refrigerator closed to preserve food
  • Use flashlights, not candles, to reduce fire risk

  1. Indoor shelter drill with roll-call
