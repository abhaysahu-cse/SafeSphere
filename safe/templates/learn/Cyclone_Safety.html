🌪 Hurricane / Cyclone Safety – Complete Learning Module

1. What Students Must Learn (Clear Theory)

⭐ A. What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a very powerful storm with strong winds, heavy rain, and flooding.
Also called Cyclone in India, Typhoon in Asia.

Hurricanes can damage buildings, trees, and cause water flooding.

Show diagram concepts:
  • Eye of hurricane
  • Rainbands
  • Wind circulation

🌤 2. BEFORE a Hurricane – Preparation & Prevention

A. Home Preparation

  • Close windows and doors securely.
  • Bring inside loose items like plants, toys, bicycles.
  • Turn off electricity/gas if instructed.
  • Charge phones & power banks.
  • Store 3 days of food, water & medicines.
  • Prepare an Emergency Kit.

B. Emergency Kit Items

  • Water bottles, dry food
  • First aid kit
  • Flashlight + batteries
  • Whistle, powerbank
  • Important documents in waterproof bag
  • Raincoat, clothes

C. Family Communication Plan

  • Know emergency phone numbers
  • Decide meeting point
  • Know nearest shelter location

D. In School

  • Discuss safety rules
  • Teacher creates evacuation map
  • Conduct mock drill yearly

🌪 3. DURING a Hurricane – Safety Actions

⭐ If Indoors

  • Stay inside in an interior room
  • Away from glass & windows
  • Unplug electronics
  • Use blankets for protection

⭐ If Outdoors

  • Stay away from trees, poles
  • Move to strong building
  • Avoid rivers, beaches, low areas

⭐ Evacuation

  • Follow instructions calmly
  • Carry emergency kit
  • Take safe route

🌧 4. AFTER a Hurricane

  • Do not go outside until allowed
  • Avoid flood water
  • Do not touch fallen wires
  • Stay away from damaged buildings
  • Throw spoiled food
  • Help elders and children
