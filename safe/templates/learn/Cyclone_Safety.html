1. What is a Cyclone / Hurricane?

Cyclone (also called hurricane or typhoon depending on region) is a large rotating storm system that brings powerful winds, heavy rain, storm surge and flooding. It forms over warm ocean water and can move inland causing extensive damage.

  • Eye: calm center
  • Rainbands: spiralling bands of thunderstorms
  • Wind circulation: rotates due to Coriolis effect

2. Causes & Risk Factors

  • Warm ocean water fuels the storm
  • Low atmospheric pressure at the center
  • Strong winds and dry land conditions increase damage risk

3. Before the Cyclone — Preparation

Home Preparation

  • Store 3 days of water, food, and medicines
  • Secure loose objects outside; bring indoors
  • Charge phones and power banks; keep torches and batteries ready
  • Know evacuation routes and nearest shelters
  • Keep vehicle fuel at least half full if evacuation likely

Emergency Kit

Water (1–2 L per person/day)
Dry food
First aid
Torch & batteries
Important documents (waterproof)
Masks / raincoats
Sign up for local official alerts (SMS, radio) and follow instructions from authorities.

4. During the Cyclone — Stay Safe

If indoors

  • Move to an interior, windowless room
  • Stay away from glass, doors and windows
  • Turn off gas and electricity only if instructed and if safe
  • Keep listening to official sources for updates

If outdoors / in a vehicle

  • Seek a sturdy shelter immediately
  • Avoid moving water, bridges and low-lying areas
  • Do not drive through flooded roads

Evacuation guidance

  • Evacuate promptly if ordered — do not wait
  • Carry the emergency kit, IDs and medicines
  • Assist children, elderly, and persons with mobility needs
