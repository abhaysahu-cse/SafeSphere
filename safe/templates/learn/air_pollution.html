1. Understanding AQI

AQI measures air pollution based on PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, O₃.

PM2.5 particles are 30x smaller than human hair and enter bloodstream.

2. AQI Levels & Risk

  • 0–50: Good
  • 51–100: Moderate
  • 101–200: Poor
  • 201–300: Very Poor
  • 301+: Severe / Emergency

3. Health Impact

  • Short-term: Eye irritation, cough, breathlessness
  • Long-term: Asthma, heart disease, lung damage

4. Emergency Response Plan

Stay indoors, avoid exercise, wear masks if outdoor exposure is unavoidable.

5. Indoor Air Safety

Indoor AQI can be improved using HEPA purifiers and wet mopping.

6. Special Care Groups

  • Children & elderly
  • Pregnant women
  • Asthma & heart patients
  • Outdoor workers
