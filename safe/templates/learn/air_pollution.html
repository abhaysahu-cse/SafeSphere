1. Understanding AQI

AQI (Air Quality Index) measures pollution levels including PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, O₃. Higher values mean greater health risk.

Critical Fact: PM2.5 particles can enter bloodstream and damage lungs, heart, and brain.

2. AQI Levels & Risk

  • 0–50: Good
  • 51–100: Moderate
  • 101–200: Poor
  • 201–300: Very Poor
  • 301+: Severe / Emergency

3. Emergency Response

During Severe AQI: Stay indoors, avoid exercise, wear masks if outdoors.

Children, elderly, and asthma patients must take extra precautions.

4. After AQI Emergency

Ventilate homes only when AQI improves. Continue hydration and monitor symptoms like breathlessness.
