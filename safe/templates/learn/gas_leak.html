1. Understanding Gas Leaks

Gas leaks involve uncontrolled release of flammable or toxic gases such as LPG, methane, ammonia, or chlorine. These gases can displace oxygen or ignite explosively.

Key Fact: LPG is heavier than air and settles near the floor, making low areas extremely dangerous.

2. Early Warning Signs

  • Strong odor (rotten egg / chemical smell)
  • Hissing sound near pipes or cylinders
  • Dizziness, nausea, breathing difficulty
  • Dead plants or animals near leak zone

3. Immediate Survival Actions

Golden Rule: Do NOT use electrical switches, mobile phones, lighters, or vehicles.
  • Turn off gas supply if safe
  • Open doors and windows
  • Evacuate immediately to fresh air
  • Move upwind and uphill

4. First Aid & Medical Response

Inhalation: Move victim to fresh air, loosen clothing, begin CPR if needed.
Burns: Cool with water, cover with clean cloth, seek hospital care.
