1. What is a Gas Leak?

A gas leak occurs when flammable or toxic gases escape from pipelines, cylinders, or industrial systems. Common gases include LPG, methane, ammonia, chlorine, and carbon monoxide.

Important: Even a small spark can trigger a massive explosion.

2. Common Causes

  • Loose or damaged gas pipes
  • Faulty regulators and valves
  • Poor industrial maintenance
  • Improper cylinder handling

3. Warning Signs

  • Strong unusual smell
  • Hissing or whistling sound
  • Dizziness or eye irritation
  • Dead plants near pipelines

4. What To Do During a Gas Leak

❌ Do NOT use switches, phones, flames, or vehicles.
  • Shut off gas supply if safe
  • Open doors and windows
  • Evacuate immediately
  • Move upwind & uphill

5. After the Leak

  • Do not re-enter until declared safe
  • Report to authorities
  • Inspect and repair system
  • Conduct safety review

6. First Aid

Inhalation: Fresh air, CPR if unconscious.
Burns: Cool with water, cover, hospital care.
