1. What is Civil Defence?

Civil defence refers to protective actions taken by civilians during war, missile strikes, or armed conflict to reduce injury, panic, and loss of life.

Core Principle: Civilians must avoid combat zones and strictly follow official government instructions.

2. Shelter Types & Selection

  • Underground shelters: Basements, bunkers
  • Interior rooms: No windows, reinforced walls
  • Public shelters: Government-marked safe zones

3. Missile Strike / Air Raid Response

Immediate Action: Move to shelter, lie flat, protect head and neck, stay away from windows.

Remain silent, conserve phone battery, and wait for official clearance.

4. Evacuation & Documentation Safety

  • Carry ID, passport, certificates
  • Keep documents waterproof
  • Evacuate only via approved routes

5. Survival Behaviour (Non-Combat)

Avoid filming attacks, spreading rumors, or approaching military areas.

6. Early Warning Systems & Sirens

Governments use sirens, SMS alerts, radio broadcasts, and mobile notifications to warn civilians about air raids or missile threats.

  • 🚨 Continuous siren: Immediate danger – take shelter
  • 📻 Radio alerts: Follow official instructions
  • 📱 Mobile alerts: Location-based warnings
Always trust official government alerts over social media.

7. Psychological Safety & Panic Control

Panic can be as dangerous as physical threats during conflict situations. Staying calm helps you make correct decisions.

  • Take slow, deep breaths
  • Reassure children and elderly
  • Limit exposure to disturbing news

8. After the Attack: What To Do

  • Wait for official “all clear” announcements
  • Check yourself and others for injuries
  • Avoid damaged buildings and unexploded objects
  • Report emergencies to authorities
Never touch suspicious objects or debris after an attack.

9. Role of Civilians in National Safety

Civil defence is a shared responsibility. Disciplined civilians help emergency services work efficiently.

  • Follow evacuation orders
  • Help vulnerable people
  • Do not spread fear or misinformation

10. Chemical, Biological & Nuclear (CBRN) Awareness

Modern conflicts may involve chemical, biological, or nuclear hazards. Civilians must recognize warning signs and take immediate protective actions.

  • ☣️ Unusual smells, mist, or sudden illness may indicate chemical exposure
  • ☢️ Nuclear threats require staying indoors and sealing rooms
  • 🦠 Biological threats spread silently—hygiene is critical
Cover nose and mouth, move indoors, and follow official decontamination instructions.

11. Home Hardening & Safe Room Preparation

Preparing a safe room inside your home increases survival chances during attacks.

  • Choose a windowless interior room
  • Reinforce doors and walls if possible
  • Store water, flashlight, radio, and first aid
Bathrooms and stairwells often provide extra structural protection.

12. Protecting Children, Elderly & Disabled Persons

Vulnerable individuals need special care during emergencies.

  • Assign one adult to each child or elderly person
  • Keep medicines and mobility aids ready
  • Explain situations calmly to reduce fear

13. Communication During Conflict

Communication networks may fail during war or missile strikes.

  • Use SMS instead of calls to save bandwidth
  • Keep emergency contacts written on paper
  • Use battery-powered radios
Avoid sharing unverified news or military movement details.

14. Legal Duties & Civilian Responsibility

Civilians are legally required to cooperate with authorities during emergencies.

  • Follow curfews and blackout rules
  • Do not obstruct rescue operations
  • Report suspicious activities responsibly
Spreading false information during conflict can be a punishable offence.
