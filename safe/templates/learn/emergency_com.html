📡 Emergency Communication Protocol

Effective reporting, dispatching, and public notification during a crisis.

The Golden Rule: Calm is Contagious

In an emergency, the speed and accuracy of information determine the outcome. Whether you are calling 112/108 or using a tactical radio, your voice must remain steady and your details precise.

1. The 5 W’s of Reporting

When reporting an incident, follow this sequence immediately:

  • WHERE: Exact location (Address, floor, or GPS).
  • WHAT: Nature of the emergency (Fire, medical, security).
  • WHO: Number of victims and descriptions of suspects.
  • WEAPONS: Are there hazards or weapons involved?
  • WHEN: Did it just happen or is it ongoing?
Stay on the line! Never hang up until the dispatcher tells you to. They may need to give you life-saving instructions.

2. Radio Etiquette (The 3 C's)

If using walkie-talkies or professional radios, remember: Clear, Concise, and Consistent.

  1. Listen before speaking: Ensure the channel is clear.
  2. Identify yourself: "Dispatch, this is Unit 1, Over."
  3. Use "Plain Talk": Avoid complex codes; use simple English unless using standard 10-codes.
Echo back instructions to confirm you understood correctly (e.g., "Received, proceeding to Sector B").

3. Public Notification (PA Systems)

When making an announcement to a crowd:

  • Speak 25% slower than usual.
  • Repeat the instruction three times.
  • Provide a clear action: "Evacuate via North Exit," not just "There is a fire."
