🚰 Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)

How communities can protect drinking water, manage sanitation in shelters, and prevent deadly water-borne diseases after floods and disasters.

Focus: Floods · Cyclones · Shelters · Disease prevention
⬅ Back to Community

Why WASH is life-saving

Contaminated water kills silently

Flood water mixes with sewage, animal waste and chemicals causing cholera, diarrhea, typhoid and hepatitis.

Poor sanitation spreads disease

Open defecation and dirty toilets in shelters spread infections rapidly, especially among children.

Simple actions save many lives

Clean water storage, handwashing stations and waste management reduce disease by more than 60%.

Recommendation: Assign one WASH coordinator per shelter and update water safety checks daily.
