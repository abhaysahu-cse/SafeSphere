🌾 Grain & Seed Banks — Build, Protect, Distribute

Community-level guidance: raised storage design, seed preservation techniques, inventory & fair distribution rules for emergency support.

Target: community leaders & extension workers • Est. reading: 20–40 min • Tools: checklist, inventory manager, distribution planner
⬅ Back to Community

Overview & Why it matters

The community grain/seed bank protects the next season's cropping potential and reduces long-term income loss after floods, droughts or pests. Priority: preserve seed quality (viability), ensure equitable rules for distribution, and keep accurate records.

Seed vs Grain

Seeds: intended for sowing. Grain: for food and sometimes saved as seed (requires treatment & testing). Treat seed more carefully—store dry, cool, and separated by variety.

Core goals

  • Keep viability high ( > 70% germination for many crops )
  • Protect from moisture, pests, heat
  • Allow quick, fair distribution after shocks

Site selection

Raised ground, well-drained, away from flood channels, with good ventilation and access for community members and vehicles.
