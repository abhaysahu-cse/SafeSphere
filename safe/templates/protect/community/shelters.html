🏚️ Shelters & Relief Management — Community

How to set up safe shelters, maintain basic supplies, organise shelter rosters and manage entry points so your community can respond quickly and safely.

Module • Community Est.time: 25–45 min (practice & drills)

Why community shelters matter

A well-prepared shelter saves lives, reduces injuries, prevents disease spread and protects essential documents, seeds and community equipment. Planning ahead reduces confusion during a disaster and accelerates recovery.

Quick summary: Choose a safe building, keep basic supplies stocked, make an access plan for people with limited mobility, and maintain a volunteer roster with clear roles.

Practice: Shelter layout (drag & drop)

Drag critical items into the shelter: water, medicines, toilets, wheelchair ramp, seed storage, sleeping mats. Aim for a layout that keeps entry clear and accessible.

Practice & Drills

Regular drills make response automatic. Focus on these exercises:

  1. Arrival drill: simulate 2-hour warning — set up shelter and receive first 10 families.
  2. Evacuation of vulnerable: practise moving elderly/disabled with wheelchairs/stretcher to safe zone.
  3. Supplies rotation: monthly check of supplies, expiry and condition.
  4. Sanitation drill: deploy emergency toilets and handwashing stations.
Template drill schedule: monthly small-scale drill; quarterly full-shelter simulation; annually invite district authority.
