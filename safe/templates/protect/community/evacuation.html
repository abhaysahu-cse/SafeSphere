Why evacuation planning matters (quick)

Most avoidable harm during floods/cyclones happens because people are delayed, routes are blocked, or mobility needs are ignored. This plan prioritises: life first → mobility & dignity → assets → vehicles.

Route criteria

  • Prefer high ground & gentle slope
  • Minimise water-crossings (bridges) & avoid electric poles
  • Wide routes (2-3 people abreast) where possible
  • Clear obstacles and mark safe entry/exits

Assembly point criteria

  • Raised, open area, away from trees/power lines
  • Space for tents, water distribution & medical desk
  • Accessible by foot and vehicles (separate lanes)
  • Signage & night reflectors

Mobility & dignity

  • Maintain a list of people who need help (with contact)
  • Wheelchair/ stretcher routes & volunteers assigned
  • Privacy (separate areas for women/families in shelters)
