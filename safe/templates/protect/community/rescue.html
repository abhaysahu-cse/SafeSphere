Why community rescue matters

When formal services are delayed, a trained local rescue team with basic boats, rope systems, lifejackets and first aid can save lives. But improper operation increases risk — this module emphasises safe procedures and simple gear suitable for low-resource settings.

Safety first

No untrained person near fast water. Always use PPE, have a shore-based safety line, and never exceed the team's capability.

Equipment focus

Quality lifejackets, sturdy ropes (not frayed), marked boats, rescue poles and a basic medical kit are high-impact buys — maintain them.

Training beats gear

A small amount of repeated practical training (boat launch, victim handling, rope rescue) reduces accidents and builds confidence.
