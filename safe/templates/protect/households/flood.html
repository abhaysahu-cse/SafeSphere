🏠 Household Flood Preparedness — Protect Home & Property

A practical, ordered plan to prepare, protect, move and recover. Focus: people first, then documents, assets, sanitation and livelihood continuity.

Module • Household • Flood Est. time: 20–40 min

Why this matters — quick orientation

Floods damage people, documents and livelihoods. A small set of prioritized actions (move people, protect seed/food, secure electricity) saves months of recovery and reduces cost.

Preparation & Planning (weeks to months)

  1. Map your house & plot: mark the highest safe points (roof/upper room) and likely entry routes for water. Photograph layout and keep copy.
  2. Household emergency plan: assign roles: who moves documents, who moves animals, who calls authorities; decide a meeting point outside flood zone.
  3. Assemble Emergency Bags: for each person: torch, powerbank, medicines, bottled water (2–3 days), photocopies of IDs, small cash, whistle, mask. Keep them in a single easy-to-grab location.
  4. Practice drills: monthly 10-minute drill to move the emergency bag and two important boxes to a high point. Keep drill records and improve each time.
  5. Materials & simple tools: collect pallets/bricks for a temporary raised platform, tarpaulin, rope, nails, hammer. Store in an accessible dry place.
  6. Communicate: exchange contact numbers and identify neighbors with boats/tractors; create a paper contact list saved in two places.

Property & Site Measures (practical, low-cost)

  • Raised storage plinth: Make a plinth 20–40 cm high for sacks; use concrete, bricks or pallets. For low-budget option: stack sealed pallets + tarpaulin.
  • Electrical safety: raise sockets and distribution boards if you renovate (60–90 cm over likely flood level); install RCCB/MCB and label circuits.
  • Floor finishes: use tiles or cement on ground floors to allow easy cleaning after floods — avoid gypsum boards.
  • Gutters & downpipes: clear before monsoon to prevent roof overflow near foundations.
  • Temporary doors: keep plywood panels and tarpaulin to block or seal doors temporarily from inside.

Drainage & water control (simple how-to)

  1. Keep drains clear: remove debris from household drains, gutters and field drains each month.
  2. Make a diversion trench: 50–100cm wide and 20–40cm deep along the property boundary to guide runoff away from structures.
  3. Percolation pits: 1m × 1m × 1m filled with stone/rubble to absorb runoff near soakaway points (good for small plots).
  4. Community ponds / check dams: coordinate with neighbors and local extension (KVK) for upstream retention works to reduce flood peaks.

Before Flood — Ordered, prioritized actions

  1. Act on first warning: begin moving people and high-priority items when an official alert arrives — do not wait until water is at the gate.
  2. People first: move elderly & children to upper floors or planned high ground; check medicines and mobility aids.
  3. Seed & food: move sacks to raised platform or upper floor; place on sealed pallets and cover with tarpaulin.
  4. Documents: move passports, insurance, land papers into sealed plastic box and keep at highest safe point; keep digital photos in cloud/phones.
  5. Electricals: unplug sensitive devices and move inverters/powerbanks higher; only switch off main if safe and dry to do so.
  6. Animals: move small livestock to a pre-identified high place; tag animals and note owners/contacts.
  7. Secure loose objects: bring pots, furniture and tools inside or tie down to prevent them becoming hazards.

During Flood — Safety & Damage control

  • Human safety: move people to the highest safe indoor area or to a community shelter; wear life-jackets where available.
  • Avoid moving water: do not walk/drive through moving water; even shallow fast water can carry people/vehicles away.
  • Switch off power safely: only operate the main switch if it can be reached without standing in water — otherwise wait for certified personnel.
  • Keep drinking water safe: use bottled/boiled water; treat water with chlorine tablets if necessary.
  • Temporary protection: cover mattresses, electronics and documents with tarpaulin and move as high as possible.
  • Record damage: photograph and time-stamp water levels and any damage for relief and insurance claims (use waterproof pouch for phone).

After Flood — Recovery & Repair

  1. Return safely: only when authorities say it is safe. Watch for structural damage, slippery floors, animals, or fallen power lines.
  2. Document all damage: photos, short videos, and a written list with approximate values for relief/insurance.
  3. Drying grain & seeds: spread thin on clean tarpaulin in direct sun, turn frequently; discard moulded seed and coordinate with KVK for seed replacement if needed.
  4. Sanitize & disinfect: clean floors, walls, and utensils, disinfect water sources if contaminated (consult local health authority).
  5. Check electrical systems: do not switch on the mains until an electrician inspects wiring and appliances.
  6. Soil & garden: remove silt in a controlled way, test for salinity and restore soil with gypsum/organic matter as recommended by local extension.

Livestock — protect animals & feed

  • Plan transport for animals beforehand: small boat, tractor trailer or neighbour arrangement.
  • Keep veterinary kit and contact details for local vet and record serial numbers of tags.
  • Mark animals with owner name/phone (temporary paint / tag) if evacuated to shelter.
  • Store at least 3 days of feed in waterproof sacks and avoid using contaminated feed after floods.

Sanitation & Health (critical)

  • Use boiled or treated water until the mains is declared safe. Keep chlorine tablets or water filters accessible.
  • Disinfect flooded latrines and avoid open defecation; set temporary latrine plan for sheltering families.
  • Seek medical attention for wounds; tetanus prophylaxis may be needed — consult clinic.

Community Actions

  • Prepare community high-points (school/temple) with basic supplies & sleeping space.
  • Maintain a boat/tractor roster and train volunteers in basic rescue and first aid.
  • Set up a community seed/food bank on a raised platform with an owner registry to help rapid recovery.
