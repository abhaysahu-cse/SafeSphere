Why heatwaves are a household threat

Heatwaves cause dehydration, heatstroke, and worsen chronic conditions. At household level they also damage perishable goods, electronics, and raise costs (cooling, water). Small prioritised steps protect people first and then assets.

Key idea: prioritise people (hydration, shade), then property (cool storage, shade for equipment), and then livelihoods (gardens, livestock). Most benefits are low-cost.

Frequently asked

How much water is enough during a heatwave?
Carry and sip water regularly; for most adults ~2–3 litres/day in normal weather — during extreme heat increase intake and include oral rehydration (sips with small salt/sugar) for labourers. Adjust for age, activity, and health conditions.
Is fan or AC better?
Fans help below ~35°C but risk increasing heat stress if air is very hot & humid. Shade, ventilation and cool roofs are primary strategies. Use AC judiciously and prefer communal cooling centres if household AC is unavailable.

Quick actions

  • Identify the coolest room and create a shaded 'cool corner'.
  • Keep an emergency water jerry (clean) and list of vulnerable neighbours.
  • Schedule strenuous work for early morning or late evening.
