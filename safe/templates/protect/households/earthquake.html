Why this matters: damage & life-loss reduction

Earthquakes can occur without warning. Most household casualties come from collapse, falling objects, and fire after shaking. Thoughtful preparation — safe building, anchored furniture, a practiced escape — reduces injuries and months of recovery.

Key principle: protect people first (safe spots, family plan), then critical assets (documents, water, electricity) and then property (retrofit & drains).

Frequently asked

Can I retrofit cheaply?
Yes. Low-cost steps (strapping water heaters, anchoring furniture, adding bracing to pantry walls, tying down heavy appliances) significantly reduce risk. For structural retrofit, consult local engineer or government scheme.
Is 'doorway protection' recommended?
No — modern advice is Drop, Cover, Hold (under table). Doorways are not safer in many modern homes. Use a sturdy table to protect yourself from falling objects.

Quick actions

  • Identify safe spots: under sturdy tables, against interior walls away from windows.
  • Secure heavy furniture, water heaters and gas cylinders.
  • Have an emergency kit with medicines, cash, copies of IDs and bottled water.
