Overview — Convert safely, quickly

When a school is used as an emergency shelter, the priority order is: Safety → Dignity → Health → Water & Sanitation → Livelihood continuity. This module guides you through planning, physical layout and daily management with minimal disruption to school property where possible.

Remember: always check with local authorities and ensure layered consent and security (school management + local disaster committee).

Case studies & tips

  • Temporary latrine arrangement: raised pit latrines with nearby handwashing reduced diarrhoeal cases in a village shelter.
  • Sleeping layout: simple 1m spacing between mats and gender-separated rows with visible signage preserved privacy and dignity.
  • Infection control: isolation corner for symptomatic persons prevented spread of respiratory illness in a shelter hosting 120 people.

Essential Quick Checklist

  • Count capacity: available classrooms & floor area
  • Set safe sleeping capacity at 1 person per 3.5–4 m² (adjust for bedding)
  • Identify water source & handwashing points
  • Designate isolation room & first-aid point
  • Assign shelter manager & roster
