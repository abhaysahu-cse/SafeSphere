Preparation & Structural checks

Look for signs of structural distress, cracks, water penetration and safe exit routes. Document with photos if possible.

Roof, windows & doors

Check roofing sheets, fasteners, window latches and protective shutters. Loose roofing is high-risk in storms and during evacuation.

Electrical, LPG & fuel safety

Faulty wiring and fuel sources cause fires and electrocutions — check switches, earthing, and shut-off points.

Water, sanitation & hygiene (WASH)

Ensure safe drinking water, functioning toilets, and emergency handwashing for sheltering people.

Assembly areas & evacuation routes

Identify safe open areas, calculate capacity, mark routes, and check signage and lighting for night evacuation.

Supplies, records & communications

First-aid, medicines, tents/sleeping mats, food stocks, phone power banks and printed contact lists are essential.

Final report & recommended actions

Write summary recommendations and a prioritized "fix list" with responsible persons and rough costs.

Tip: Use photos to document urgent items. Share exported JSON with local engineers or authorities for faster assistance.
